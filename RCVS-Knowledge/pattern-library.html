Pattern Library

Veterinary Evidence’s pattern library is a collection of design elements, materials, and guidelines currently in use on the website. The pattern library is a snapshot resulting from the redesign project that concluded in December 2023. One of the purposes of this collection and overview is to facilitate future design improvements to the site.

As such, Veterinary Evidence’s pattern library is updated in tandem with design changes to the website. The overview is focused on different key areas that are nonetheless closely linked and influence each other:

  • Logos
  • Layout
  • Colour
  • Typography
  • Buttons
  • Iconography
  • Imagery

 

Logos

Veterinary Evidence is closely associated with RCVS Knowledge and as such there are many visual cues to this proximity in the logo itself. Besides having a Veterinary Evidence logo in its various formats, vector or bitmap, for digital and print use, we also have a condensed version which can be used when space is limited.

Layout

We have adopted a workspace with 1320px wide, divided into 12 columns with 88px and gutters of 24px. This layout grid is fluid and it utilises Bootstrap's core framework to allow the design to respond down to small screens in a smooth fashion.

The homepage's top carousel breaks out of the main container. Due to its prime position, the information in the carousel stands out as a way for the reader to begin to immerse herself in readings of knowledge summaries, articles, and such.

Information pages utilise 9 columns out of 12, with text left aligned. This narrower space allows us to have a smaller number of characters per line (include link to section in typography) for accesibility reasons and to allow a more comfortable reading.

 

Colour

Veterinary Evidence colourspace exists in continuity with the main RCVS Knowledge colourspace. As such 'Knowledge purple' is adopted in variations.

 
 
 
 

 

Typography

Veterinary Evidence uses two typefaces which exist in continuity with our core brand guidelines at RCVS Knowledge and RCVS, more broadly. As such we have adopted Adobe Caslon Pro and Montserrat.

Adobe Caslon Pro
Caslon Pro is used for all RCVS work associated with prestige events such as Royal College Day or Fellowship Day. It is also used on certificates and other honours and awards.

"William Caslon released his first typefaces in 1722. Caslon’s types were based on seventeenth-century Dutch old style designs, which were then used extensively in England. Because of their remarkable practicality, Caslon’s designs met with instant success. For her Caslon revival, designer Carol Twombly studied specimen pages printed by William Caslon between 1734 and 1770. The OpenType Pro version merges formerly separate fonts (expert, etc.), and adds both central European language support and several additional ligatures. Ideally suited for text in sizes ranging from 6- to 14-point, Adobe Caslon Pro is the right choice for magazines, journals, book publishing, and corporate communications." - More information about this typeface can be found on Adobe Fonts.
